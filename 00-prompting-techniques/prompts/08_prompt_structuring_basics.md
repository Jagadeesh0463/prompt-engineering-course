# Assignment 08 — Prompt Structuring Basics

## Task
Beginner Fitness Routine for a Home-based Office Worker

## Why this is Structured Prompting
This prompt is divided into 5 clear sections: Role, Task, Context, Constraints, and Output Format.
Each section removes ambiguity so the model knows exactly who it is, what to produce,
for whom, within what limits, and in what structure.

## Prompt

Role:
You are a beginner-friendly certified fitness coach.

Task:
Create a 7-day beginner fitness routine for someone who has never exercised before.

Context:
The person is a 25-year-old office worker who sits for long hours.
They can spare only 30 minutes per day for exercise.
They have no gym membership and will exercise at home.
Their goal is to improve energy levels and build a basic fitness habit.

Constraints:
- No gym equipment required
- Keep exercises simple and easy to follow
- Do not use fitness jargon
- Each day must fit within 30 minutes
- Include one rest day

Output Format:
Return the plan in this format:

Day 1: [Focus Area] — [List of exercises with duration]
Day 2: [Focus Area] — [List of exercises with duration]
Day 3: [Focus Area] — [List of exercises with duration]
Day 4: REST DAY — Light stretching or walking only
Day 5: [Focus Area] — [List of exercises with duration]
Day 6: [Focus Area] — [List of exercises with duration]
Day 7: [Focus Area] — [List of exercises with duration]

## Model Response

Response:
--------------------------------------------------
Hello there! It's fantastic that you're ready to start this journey to better energy
and a healthy habit. This plan is designed specifically for you — a 25-year-old office
worker who sits a lot, wants more energy, and is just starting out.
We'll keep it simple, effective, and right in your own home!

Day 1: Full Body Foundations — Get familiar with basic movements.
- Warm-up (5 min): Marching in Place, Arm Circles, Gentle Leg Swings, Torso Twists
- Workout (20 min): Chair Squats, Wall Push-ups, Knee Plank, Marching in Place
  (30 sec each, 30 sec rest, 3 rounds)
- Cool-down (5 min): Overhead Arm Stretch, Gentle Hamstring Stretch, Child's Pose

Day 2: Core & Energy Boost — Focus on strengthening your core.
- Warm-up (5 min): Marching in Place, Arm Circles, Shoulder Rolls, Torso Twists
- Workout (20 min): Glute Bridges, Bird-Dog, Standing Side Bends, Stepping Side-to-Side
  (30 sec each, 30 sec rest, 3 rounds)
- Cool-down (5 min): Gentle Quad Stretch, Cat-Cow Stretch

Day 3: Full Body Strength & Mobility — Building on foundational strength.
- Warm-up (5 min): Marching in Place, Leg Swings, Arm Circles, Shoulder Rolls
- Workout (20 min): Chair Squats, Wall Push-ups, Calf Raises, High Knees Marching
  (45 sec each, 30 sec rest, 2 rounds)
- Cool-down (5 min): Chest Stretch, Overhead Arm Stretch, Gentle Neck Tilts

Day 4: REST DAY — Light stretching or walking only.
Today is for recovery. Take a 15-30 minute walk or do gentle stretches from Day 1 or Day 2.

Day 5: Lower Body & Posture Focus — Strengthening legs to counteract long sitting.
- Warm-up (5 min): Marching in Place, Arm Circles, Leg Swings, Torso Twists
- Workout (20 min): Modified Lunges, Superman, Chair Dips, Gentle Step Jacks
  (30 sec each, 30 sec rest, 3 rounds)
- Cool-down (5 min): Gentle Hamstring Stretch, Lower Back Twist

Day 6: Full Body Endurance — Increasing work time to build stamina.
- Warm-up (5 min): Marching in Place, Shoulder Rolls, Arm Circles, Leg Swings
- Workout (20 min): Chair Squats, Wall Push-ups, Knee Plank, Brisk Marching
  (45 sec each, 30 sec rest, 2 rounds)
- Cool-down (5 min): Quad Stretch, Overhead Arm Stretch, Cat-Cow Stretch

Day 7: Gentle Full Body & Flexibility — Review progress and focus on stretching.
- Warm-up (5 min): Marching in Place, Arm Circles, Leg Swings, Torso Twists
- Workout (20 min): Glute Bridges, Standing Torso Twists, Lying Leg Raises, Cat-Cow
  (45 sec each, 30 sec rest, 2 rounds)
- Cool-down (5 min): Child's Pose, Gentle Neck Stretches, Full Body Stretch

Congratulations on completing your first week! Consistency is key.
Keep up the great work — you've got this!
